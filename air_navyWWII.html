Air and Navy WWII

1) Fighters/ Machine Guns

Aerial reconnaissance and artillery spotting were important to successful missions and it quickly became clear that having control of the airspace gave troops a better advantage over the enemy. Originally, pilots and observers (anyone else on the plane) would shoot down enemy planes with shotguns, pistols, and rifles which was ineffective. The solution was machine guns. In 1913 the Vickers company in Britain had built the very first fighter plane, F.B.5 Gambus, which was armed with a machine gun and had the propeller moved behind the engine instead of infront of it. In 1915 the plane was entered into service as the first plane designed for air-to-air combat. Although the fighters were ultimately a success, they became heavy because of the gun and slow because of the placement of the rudders and engine. Their success was mostly due to accidental encounters with the enemy.
